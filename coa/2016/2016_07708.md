{**28 NY3d at 1048} OPINION OF THE COURT

Memorandum.

The order of the Appellate Division should be affirmed. There is record support for the conclusion that the nearly 4ï¿½ year delay between the crime and the indictment "did not deprive defendant of his due process right to prompt prosecution" ([3]People v [*2]Velez, 22 NY3d 970, 972 [2013]). This is not a circumstance where "a lengthy and unjustifiable delay in commencing the prosecution . . . require[s] dismissal even though no actual prejudice to the defendant is shown" (People v Singer, 44 NY2d 241, 253-254 [1978]).

Chief Judge DiFiore and Judges Pigott, Rivera, Abdus-Salaam, Stein, Fahey and Garcia concur.{**28 NY3d at 1049}

On review of submissions pursuant to section 500.11 of the Rules of the Court of Appeals (22 NYCRR 500.11), order affirmed, in a memorandum.
